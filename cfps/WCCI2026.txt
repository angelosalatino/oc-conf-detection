Call for Position Papers
New Position Paper Tracks at WCCI 2026 
We are pleased to announce the introduction of Position Paper Tracks at IEEE WCCI. In the dynamic and increasingly impactful field of computational intelligence, progress is not only achieved by novel algorithms and empirical results, but also from forward-thinking perspectives, critical reflections, and calls to action across theory, methodology, applications, societal impact, and interdisciplinary cross-fertilisation. While traditional conference papers focus on new methods, empirical results, benchmark studies, and applications, position papers emphasise viewpoints: why certain directions matter (or don t), how the field should evolve, what emerging challenges or opportunities we face, what are the intersections with other domains (e.g., machine learning, optimisation, operations research, industry, sustainability), and how we might act collectively. 
The new tracks are designed to provide a dedicated forum for sharing thought-provoking viewpoints, inspiring debate, and shaping future directions in computational Intelligence. For further detail on the specific guidelines for each of the three conferences, see the dedicated pages for IJCNN, CEC, and Fuzz-IEEE (below).
Submission Guidelines 
* Position papers must use the same general formatting and submission system as regular WCCI papers (e.g., same page limit, template, submission portal). 
* Titles must begin with the phrase  Position Paper:   to clearly identify submissions to this track.
* All submissions will undergo double-blind peer review. Authors should anonymise their manuscripts accordingly.
* Submissions must adhere to the same policies as regular WCCI papers, including ethics, dual submission, and plagiarism policies.
* Accepted position papers will be presented at WCCI (either via oral or poster format) and will be included in the conference proceedings (e.g., IEEE Xplore) and indexed accordingly. 
Evaluation Criteria 
Position papers will be evaluated based on: 
* Position: Does the paper clearly articulate a stance, argument or perspective relevant to evolutionary computation?
* Support: Is the position backed by clear reasoning, relevant evidence, and appropriate citations of the literature? 
* Significance: Is the topic timely, impactful, and relevant to the WCCI audience? 
* Discussion potential: Does the paper inspire constructive and meaningful discussion among researchers, practitioners or educators in EC? 
* Communication quality: Is the paper well organised, coherent and clearly written? 
* Context: Does the paper situate its perspective within existing literature, identify gaps, and propose a way forward? 
Papers that are essentially standard empirical research papers (rather than viewpoint/position-type) but are submitted as Position Papers are likely to be rejected as out-of-scope. 
Important Dates 
* All regular deadlines for paper submission, notification, etc. apply to Position Papers.
